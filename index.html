THE PROBLEM

help, I can’t find enough people..

You may know the struggle. You want to do a certain sport but you just don’t have enough friends or know enough people - especially when it comes to team sports. The search for teammates can be difficult. And maybe thinking about joining an already established sports or friends group can be intimidating if talking to strangers isn’t your greatest strength.




THE SOLUTION

we want to make it easy!

This problem at hand led us to think about a way to make the search for other teammates more easy, simple and flexible . We wanted to design an app that makes looking for other players easy while taking away the pressure of joining an already established sports & friends group or community.

EMPHATHISE

understand the user

This phase was a journey of discovery with the aim to learn more about the people who struggle with finding enough teammtes to do sports with. Ultimately the goal for us was to identify who this product is for, what their problem is and understanding what these people actually do. It’s all about understanding the user, their struggles and their needs.

To achieve this, we conducted six user interviews. All of the participants are potential candidates for this app and display some kind of physical activity in their daily lives with the level of activity varying from going to the gym once a week to participating in a sports club 4 times a week.

introduction

• What kind of sport are you doing?
• How long have you been doing it?

topic specific

• Who do you usually do sports with?
• How often do you do sports a week?
• How are you looking for other players when you do?
• Have you ever thought of using online options in order to find other players?
• Would you like explore new and different sports activities from the one you are currently doing?
• Are you using any kind of sport offering?
• Are you a member of online communities?
• Tell me about your last experience when intending to do sports with your friends.
• Have you made friends through sports?
• Have you done sports because of certain friendships?
• What expectations would you have for an app for online sports communities?

user stories

The answers provided in the interview were used to build two scenarios that reflect and sum up the main issues that the participants face when trying to do a certain sport.

variety in activities

01

Joey is an fitness enthusiast and enjoys going to the gym 5 days a week. He is currently trying to lose body fat to show off his hard earned muscles in summer 2021. While he really enjoys lifting weights, he hates the thought of doing cardio to burn the extra calories needed to burn fat. Since he is also really into volleyball, he tries to convince his friends to play volleyball with him so that he can substitue his usual treadmill run with a more fun and exciting activity.

everyone is busy

02

Kevin is visiting his friends in his hometown Stuttgart for one day. He and his friends are really excited to play football just like in their childhood. When planning this activity two of their friends say that they cant participate because of work and family. To fill in this gap, they asked other acquaintances but none of them have time. Kevin and his friends now wish that they could ask strangers to play with them.

IDEATE

gathering ideas

To explore different ideas on how the users would want to interact with the app, we presented them several different design approaches in form of rough sketches using crazy 8’s and brainstorming skechtes. We then let them test the sketch prototypes and explained them the concept of each idea. After the tests were finished, we asked them to point out pros and cons in each of the designs. The preferred design was then expanded on and developed through iterations of low fidelity mockups and usability tests in the design phase.

DESIGN

low fidelity prototype

Some ideas were well received, others were disregarded. We took the feedback from the useres and created based on the insights a low fidelity (lo-fi) prototype. It allowed us to discover eventual flaws in the usability and let us validate the design of the app without being distracted by colors and shapes.

We then proceeded with moderated usability tests to validate the design. The goal here was to understand how the user interacts with the app. It’s about understanding the problem, not validating the solution.

The user feedback from previous usability tests were now taken into account when finalising the design. We created a high fidelity (hi-fi) prototype to test our design once again. REFLECTION

final thoughts

Working on this Case Study for our university course -Digital Storytelling- helped us to learn about all the facettes of UX Design and improved our coordination skills as we needed to be in constant communication throughout the project. From applying our theoretical knowledge gained throughout our bachelor’s degree and online research, to learning how to conduct interviews. to being patient with users who’ve never see a prototype in their life. All these experiences helped us further strengthen our foundation in user centered design. We also learned to really appreciate all the small little design choices designers make and programmers code that go unnoticed by the regular user.
